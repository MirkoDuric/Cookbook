Hurmašice Preparation Method

    You can make this recipe one day prior Soaking time 1 hour or overnight
  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (180°C fan-forced). Brush two large shallow oven trays with oil to grease.
    2. For the Syrup
  2. Combine the sugar, water, lemon juice, cloves and vanilla bean paste in a medium saucepan and stir over medium heat until the sugar dissolves
  3. Bring to boil, reduce the heat to lowest possible heat and simmer gently, for 35 minutes or until the syrup thickens and reduces to about 900 ml.
    4. Meanwhile, For the Dough
  4. Use an electric mixer to beat the butter, sugar and orange zest until pale and creamy
  5. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition until well combined and smooth
  6. Sift together flour and baking powder into a bowl, then stir in the semolina
  7. Add to butter mixture and use a wooden spoon and then your hands to mix to a soft dough
  8. Roll about a tablespoonful of the dough into a log about and 2 cm wide and 6.5 cm long
  9. Press the log lightly onto the small hole-side of a box grater to flatten slightly to form a biscuit about 4 cm x 7 cm and about 1 cm thick and to create a pattern on the surface
  10. Gently peel the dough away form the grater and then use your hands to round the ends slightly to form an oval if needed
  11. Place on the greased baking tray
  12. Repeat with the remaining dough, placing about 2 cm apart
  13. Bake the biscuits for about 20 minutes, swapping the trays half way through baking until golden brown and cooked through
  14. Pour the hot syrup over the hot biscuits
  15. Set aside until the biscuits soak up most of the syrup (this will take about 30 minutes)
    16. Notes
  16. These biscuits will keep in an airtight container in the fridge for 2 weeks, with the syrup spooned over
  17. Serve straight from the fridge or at room temperature
