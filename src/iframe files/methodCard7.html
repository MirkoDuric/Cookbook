Lasagne Preparation Method

  1. Preheat the oven to160C/325F/Gas 3.
    2. For the Ragu
  2. Heat a large frying pan until hot and add the oil
  3. Cook the mince until browned all over
  4. Remove from the heat and transfer to a plate
  5. Add the onion, celery (if using) and garlic to the pan and cook until softened
  6. Return the meat to the pan and stir in the flour
  7. Add the stock and bring to the boil
  8. Add the redcurrant jelly (or sugar), tomato purée and thyme, then stir well.
  9. Stir in the canned tomatoes
  10. Bring to the boil again, cover and simmer in the oven for 1-½ hours, or until the beef is tender
    11. For the White Sauce
  11. Melt the butter in a saucepan
  12. Add the flour and cook over the heat for one minute
  13. Gradually whisk in the hot milk, whisking until thickened
  14. Add the Dijon mustard and parmesan cheese
  15. Season well with salt and pepper
    16. For the Lasagne
  16. Put one third of the meat sauce in the base of a 2.3 litre/4 pint shallow ovenproof dish
  17. Spoon one third of the white sauce on top
  18. Arrange one layer of lasagne sheets on top
  19. Season
  20. Spoon half of the remaining meat sauce on top and then half of the white sauce
  21. Put another layer of lasagne sheets on top
  22. Add the remaining meat sauce and remaining white sauce
  23. Sprinkle with the cheddar cheese
  24. Leave for six hours before cooking so that the pasta can start to soften
  25. Preheat the oven temperature to 200C/400F/Gas 6
  26. Cook in the middle of the oven for about 45 minutes- or until golden brown on top, bubbling around the edges and the pasta is soft
